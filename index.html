Cipher

A cipher is the method of shifting the sequence of letters by a set number. Originated in ancient Rome with the "caesar cipher". A variant is the "Rot13" which shifts a letter by 13 places.

Used as a very weak encoding so the answer is not immediately decipherable. Not recommended for any actual security.

Morse

A communication method, converting characters into a series of dots and dashes.

Used in the navy to transmit messages in a series of sounds.


Known Bugs

  • Morse currently won't decode.
  • Rot won't decode unless the number to decode by is 13.
  • No sound option for Morse.
